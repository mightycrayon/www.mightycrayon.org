/ Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What is UX research?

    +

    UX (User Experience) research is to understand the needs of the target market for a system or product. It clarifies who your customers are, how they will use your product or service, and what they eventually require from you, in addition to assisting you in understanding the issue you're attempting to solve.

    Once this is done, products, services, or software may be designed more effectively.

  2. Why do I need a UX research?

    +

    UX research is essential, the design strategy and choices made at each stage of the design process must be informed by UX research. The information gathered from UX research aids in organizing ideas and features, crafting user narratives, and guiding decisions about the functionality and aesthetic of the product.

  3. What's the difference between UI and UX?

    +

    User Experience (UX) relates to the user's experience with a product or service, whereas User Interface (UI) refers to the visual components by which people interact with a product.

    Therefore, UX focuses on the user and their experience with the product, whereas UI focuses on the visual interface elements such as font, colors, menu bars, and more.

    Read: UI + UX: Two Different but Complimenting Practices

  4. What is a Design system and Why do I need it?

    +

    A design system is a coherently structured collection of interrelated patterns and common behaviors. Design systems support the creation of digital products, such as applications or websites.

    The main advantage of design systems is their speedy replication of designs using preset UI elements and components. Teams might reuse the same components repeatedly to avoid having to come up with new ideas and running the risk of unintended inconsistency.

  5. What is a Prototype?

    +

    A prototype is an early version of a product that has been produced to test a theory or procedure. It is a phrase that is employed in a number of fields, such as semantics, design, electronics, and software development.

    System analysts and users typically utilize a prototype to assess a new design that aims to increase precision.

  6. What makes a good UX?

    +

    The focus of user experience (UX) is to have a thorough awareness of people, their needs, values, abilities, and constraints. To make a good User Experience (UX) these things are must have that are useful, usable, desirable, findable, credible, and accessible.

    The goal of UX best practices is to enhance how users interact with and perceive your product and any connected services.

    Read: UX is the new SEO
